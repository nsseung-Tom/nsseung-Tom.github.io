## Working Papers

<div class="research-card">
  <h3>Reallocation Channel of a Minimum-Wage Shock</h3>
  <p class="collaborators">with Prof. Jungmin Lee (Seoul National University)</p>
  <p class="description">
    This paper investigates the labor reallocation dynamics following South Korea's substantial 
    minimum wage increases (2017‚Äì2019), which raised the hourly minimum by over 30% cumulatively. 
    Using the universe of Korea's employer‚Äìemployee matched administrative data spanning 2015‚Äì2020 
    (over <strong>300 million observations</strong>), we examine how workers reallocate across firms, 
    industries, and regions in response to minimum wage shocks.
  </p>
  <p class="description">
    Our identification strategy exploits geographic variation in exposure to the minimum wage increase, 
    employing a <strong>Difference-in-Differences</strong> design. We find that regional business cycle 
    conditions significantly moderate both the employment effects and the reallocation patterns, 
    suggesting that labor market tightness plays a crucial role in how minimum wage policies transmit 
    through the economy.
  </p>

## Ongoing Projects

<div class="research-card">
  <h3>Family-Friendly Firms and the Motherhood Penalty</h3>
  <p class="collaborators">with Tammy Lee & Prof. Jungmin Lee (Seoul National University)</p>
  <p class="description">
    The motherhood penalty‚Äîthe earnings and career disadvantages experienced by mothers compared 
    to childless women and men‚Äîremains a persistent feature of labor markets worldwide. This project 
    investigates whether and how firm-level family-friendly policies can mitigate these penalties.
  </p>
  <p class="description">
    We examine three key dimensions of workplace flexibility: <strong>flexible work arrangements</strong>, 
    <strong>on-site childcare facilities</strong>, and <strong>remote work options</strong>. 
    Using a <strong>causal forest</strong> machine learning approach, we identify heterogeneous 
    treatment effects across different worker types, industries, and firm characteristics.
  </p>
  <p class="description">
    Our findings have direct policy implications for workplace regulations and corporate family 
    support initiatives, particularly as remote work becomes more prevalent post-pandemic.
  </p>
